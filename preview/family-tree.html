Member-named strains

Strains, named by members

Every season, a member whose treatment shaped a formulation lends it their name. Below — the four current drops, the patients who named them, and what the strain was developed to address.

Marina — for sleep that arrives without negotiation

Indica-leaning, 18% THC, 8% CBD. Named by Marina, a member in care since 2024 for treatment-resistant insomnia following her cancer treatment. Developed with our prescribing team after eighteen months of titration. Released in spring jars of forty.

Tomé — for the chronic pain that other clinics could not name

Balanced THC:CBD oil, 1:1, sublingual. Named by Tomé, a member living with central sensitisation syndrome for nine years. The formulation he and Dr Marques arrived at after his fourth pain clinic gave up. Quarterly batches.

Inês — for the morning anxiety that nothing else touches

CBD-dominant, 12% CBD, 1% THC, vaporiser flower. Named by Inês, a member who came to us when SSRIs left her unable to feel anything at all. Worked with the practice for fourteen months to find a morning protocol. Winter release.

Henrique — for MS spasticity and the dignity of moving

1:1 THC:CBD oromucosal spray, pharmaceutical preparation. Named by Henrique, an architect with secondary-progressive MS, who insisted the practice carry a spray-format option after returning from a trial in Madrid. Continuous availability.

The practice

We built a practice. The membership built itself.

Family Tree was started in 2024 by Dr Elena Marques and four founder-patients she had been treating since her time at Apothicare. The conversation that became the practice happened around her kitchen table: what would a medical-cannabis service look like if patients owned a piece of it? What if the strains carried our names instead of trademark numbers? What if the doctor who started your treatment was the doctor who finished it?

We answered those questions with a small townhouse in Príncipe Real, seven clinicians, and 340 founding members. There is no waitlist. There is, as there always was, a conversation.

Membership

How to join the family

Four steps, no waitlist. Most members are in care within ten days of first writing to us.

I

Write to the practice

A short letter — the medical history you'd like us to know, what you have tried, what brought you here. We read every one. Practice, not platform.

II

A conversation

Forty-five minutes with a prescribing physician — at the townhouse, or by video if you live further than the train. The conversation is the consultation.

III

Membership card

If we are the right practice for you, we issue a numbered membership card. Yours for life. Carries your prescriber's name and direct line.

IV

The Letter and the drops

From your first issue of The Letter onward, you are part of the conversation that shapes the next strain, the next event, the next member-named jar.
