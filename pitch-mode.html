Field note · 03 / 12 · Operator interview

"It stopped being something I prompted, and started being something the team checked in with."

Six weeks into running Automatos for a 40-person ops team, Maren K. talks about the moment her relationship with the workforce changed — and what the room is doing now that nobody is asking it to.

The first three weeks felt like the demo on the website: pick an agent, give it a Slack channel, write a mission, watch the mission run. Useful, but not different — a smarter zap, basically. The shift came on a Tuesday in late April, when Maren noticed the inventory agent had filed a P2 ticket without being asked.

It hadn't been told to file tickets. It had been told to watch inventory and report anomalies in #ops-pulse. Somewhere between "report anomaly" and "this is the third time this week", it had decided the message wasn't enough and reached for the Linear skill it had inherited from the Shopify Ops pack.
